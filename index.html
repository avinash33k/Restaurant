Breakfast & Starters

Paratha
$1
Paratha is an unleavened flatbread in India made by baking flour dough on a frying pan and finishing off with shallow frying. It is layered by coating with oil and folding repeatedly using a laminated dough technique. It is the most popular Indiai food for breakfast in restaurants, which is normally eaten with Bhaji (mixed vegetable) or lentil or a miixture of these two together, and with fried eggs.
idly & vada
$3
Idli and vada are classic South Indian breakfast staples, cherished for their simplicity and taste. Idlis are steamed rice cakes made from a fermented batter of rice and lentils, resulting in a soft and fluffy texture. Vadas are savory doughnuts made from ground urad dal (black gram) and spices, deep-fried to a crispy golden brown exterior while retaining a moist interior. Served together with coconut chutney and sambar, idli and vada offer a harmonious balance of flavors and textures, making them a beloved choice for a wholesome morning meal.
Ghee Masala Dosa
$3
Ghee Masala Dosa is a popular South Indian delicacy cherished for its savory flavors and crispy texture. A thin, fermented rice and lentil crepe is spread with clarified butter (ghee) and filled with a spiced potato mixture. The dosa is then folded into a delicious parcel and cooked until golden brown, creating a delightful contrast of crunchy exterior and soft interior. Served with coconut chutney and sambar, Ghee Masala Dosa is a comforting and satisfying meal enjoyed across India and beyond.

Grilled Chicken has become very popular in India recently. You can find mouth-watering grilled chicken on almost every street. Chicken is marinated with spices and later grilled on the griller. The chicken is super moist inside but crunchy bits of chars outside make them taste heavenly. Grilled chickens are normally served with Naan Rooti (an oven-baked flatbread), mayonnaise, and simple salads. This is an evening snack, mostly available after 5.00 pm at the restaurants.

The Indian word "Bhuna Khichuri means browning, mixing, or deep frying spices with rice and lentil. It is the richer version of the plain old Khichuri. Bhuna means braised, so this dish is kind of slow-cooked to infuse the flavors and the spices as opposed to the simple process of cooking everything together durnng the other times. You cannot ignore the beautiful aroma while it is braising and cooking!
Hyderabadi Biryani
$4
Hyderabadi Biryani is a regal dish from the culinary heritage of Hyderabad, India. Fragrant basmati rice is layered with tender, marinated meat—often chicken or mutton—infused with a blend of aromatic spices like saffron, cardamom, and cloves. The cooking technique, called dum, involves slow-cooking the ingredients in a sealed pot, allowing the flavors to meld and the meat to become incredibly tender. Garnished with fried onions, fresh mint, and cilantro, each mouthful is a burst of savory delight, perfectly balancing spices and textures.

Kunafa
$3
Kunafa is a Middle Eastern dessert renowned for its decadent taste and crispy texture. It consists of shredded phyllo dough layered with a rich cheese filling, typically sweetened with sugar syrup or honey. The pastry is baked until golden brown, creating a crunchy exterior while maintaining a soft, gooey center. Garnished with crushed pistachios or almonds and a sprinkle of rosewater, kunafa offers a delightful blend of sweet and savory flavors.
Lassi
$2
Lassi is a blend of yogurt, water, spices, and sometimes fruit. Lassis are enjoyed chilled as a hot-weather refreshment, mostly taken with lunch. It is a very popular drink in India which you must try at least once while visiting India..
Falooda
$3
Falooda is a cold dessert very popular in India. Traditionally it is made from mixing rose syrup, vermicelli, sweet basil seeds, and pieces of jelly with milk often topped off with a scoop of ice cream, lastly garnished with chopped fruits.

Rasmalai is a unique Indiai delicacy. This Indiai dessert is a flattened cheese ball soaked in malai clotted cream) flavored with cardamom. Malai or clotted cream itself has a unique texture.
